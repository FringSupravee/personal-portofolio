Project Overview

Date: November 2021
Tool: Figma
Position: Researcher, Visual Designer, Product Designer

This is the final project of my Design Thinking class. We have around 2 months to work on and deliver a high-fidelity prototype as a final product. We will be judged by the FRUIT model: financial viability, rarity, usefulness & desirability, impact, and team effort & learning, which has been used by many Venture Capital to consider the sustainability of startups when funding.


We received some requirement according to the course

  • use the Design Thinking tools when solving the problem
  • Our solution must aim to achieve the 17 GOALS of Sustainable development Growth by the United Nations. It is designed to be a "blueprint for achieving a better and more sustainable future for all."


1/6 Introduction

We started off by pitching our 'How Might We' statement to other group members. We use Zen Voting to select the most popular How Might We statement. As a designer, this is the most important thing because it directs the goal and direction of this project that you will be working on.


Problem Statement

"young adult in Hong Kong struggle to find accurate information about sex and gender related information and want to find 'a safe space' that they can freely express themselves and share their stories to further discuss with professionals/ specialists. If we would be able to provide platform with resources on sex and other topics related to sex, this can be a game changer to sex education landscape in Hong Kong by giving a reliable information with non-judgmental mediators."

Our motivation
  1. We agree that to remove the taboo of openly sex-related conversation in Asia, we need to start from a young age.
  2. Due to the perception about sex in Asian countries and sex education, women have a higher rate of early pregnancy and unprotected sex than Western countries with proper sex education.
  3. We want to increase awareness about gender diversity and remove the gender norm to express themselves freely.

Moreover, our team consists of students from different countries, including Hong Kong, Taiwan, Thailand, Singapore, Poland, and Switzerland. So we agree that we can research from different beliefs and perceptions of each country and compare Europe's sex education in middle school.



2/6 User Research

We asked around our friends and classmates from different backgrounds and types of school, which we see in Asia. The national school type touched only briefly about the sex education in the biology class where the lesson is about 'human body.' The current curriculum only consisted of the reproductive system of males and females. Although there are more sex education courses in the international school, it is only one hour per week for 1 year. Fortunately, the lesson covered more than the reproduction system; the international school also touched on birth control methods like contraception or wearing a proper condom.

"In one lifetime, a person only spend 1 day to learn about Sex Education."

Our assumption is that it might result from more academic heavy of Asian schools that make the schools remove sex education. Parents also care more about the future career and academic area to care for informs the future citizens to understand puberty, child-grooming, or gender equality, which is yet to confirm.

4/6 Define, again

We know that 'removing taboo' would be too big for a 2-months project. We have research on both user and market then we want to narrow down our focus on...

  1. Remove the middleman (teacher) when teaching about sex education.
  2. We will convince parents by providing proven data about the positive result of sex education and giving them access to our course material. Which will let them understand and feel comfortable about the topic that students will learn.

Then we define our new How Might We statement...


HMW Statement
How might we create a platform for middle school students so that we can remove taboo about the sex-related topic & increase the understanding about gender diversity

5/6 Ideation

We meet up again to brainstorm for our idea. We agree that in terms of Solution, we want to create digital product that is 'free' for our users to easily access. We will use Hong Kong as our trial geographic location because it would be convenient for us to find test subject to evaluate our idea and product.

Later, we utilize the magic box method to see our program's vision. In this method, we also come up with our program's name, 'I-ed-Sex,' because we see ourselves as innovative and minimal. However, since our target users are middle school, we want to add playfulness into the box.


Core Feature
  • On-demand video - Students can learn from centralized curriculum by us and can rewatch it anytime. By the end of the lesson, students get small quiz.

  • Private chat - Students can consult with Sex Education specialist, which have option to stay anonymous or show their identity. This page is the communication channel among the mediator (teacher) and the students in class.
  • Offline classroom activities - Class activities creates to bond the students and mediator to have unorthodox activities to spice up the lesson. The activities consist of workshop, video game, virtual reality (VR).

6/6 Conclusion

The next step and vision for this Porject
  • Expand worldwide: supporting every woman in need is our goal. Hence, we want to develop and launch the app internationally. Especially, women in developing and under-developed countries can benefit from our service.
  • Partnership: as mentioned in the profitability, we want to partner with various organizations to make our solution come true. Hence, we want to shine the light and make our victims receive the opportunities to be more confident in both personal and professional direction.
